# OOH Competitive Landscape

Research date: May 3, 2026
Project: Sightline

## Bottom Line

Sightline should not position as another OOH marketplace, DSP, SSP, or measurement currency. Those categories already have mature competitors with inventory access, buying relationships, and measurement datasets.

The strongest wedge is:

> Pre-flight creative and placement intelligence for physical ads before budget is committed.

This means Sightline should sell the idea that it catches obvious OOH failures early: bad sightlines, weak readability, poor viewing angle, short exposure windows, cluttered environments, low contrast, QR infeasibility, and mismatched creative/context.

## Market Context

The market is worth pursuing. OAAA reported U.S. OOH revenue of $9.46B in 2025, up 3.6% year over year, with DOOH representing 36.3% of revenue and growing 10.5% year over year.

Source: https://oaaa.org/news/out-of-home-advertising-revenue-reaches-record-9-46-billion/

## Competitor Map

| Category | Examples | What They Are Good At | Why Not Compete Head-On |
|---|---|---|---|
| Marketplaces and managed buying | AdQuick, OneScreen.ai, BillboardsIn, Blip | Inventory search, planning, buying, managed execution, campaign measurement | Requires supply liquidity, pricing, vendor relationships, buyer trust, and campaign ops |
| Programmatic DOOH platforms | Vistar, Broadsign, Place Exchange, Hivestack, VIOOH | DSP/SSP pipes, digital screen monetization, targeting, reporting, proof-of-play | Scale game with strong incumbents and consolidation |
| OOH agencies and specialist platforms | billups, Talon, Kinetic | Strategy, planning, buying, attention, effectiveness studies, managed services | Service-heavy, relationship-driven, proprietary data advantages |
| Measurement currencies and data layers | Geopath, Ipsos/Route, Nielsen OOH, Mira | Audience/reach/frequency measurement, mobility data, market research | Sightline cannot credibly claim official measurement without partnerships |
| Verification and proof | Veridooh, StreetMetrics, media-owner proof tools | Delivery verification, proof-of-play, proof-of-posting, campaign automation, reporting | Valuable but post-campaign; Sightline is more differentiated pre-buy |
| Media owners | JCDecaux, Lamar, OUTFRONT, Clear Channel Outdoor | Owned inventory, sales channels, proprietary audience insights | They control supply; good partners/data sources, poor direct targets |

## High-Priority Competitors

### billups

Strategic threat level: high.

billups is the closest conceptual competitor because its analytics/attention products already talk about attention, audience demographics, dwell time, frequency, reach, computer vision, viewsheds, and creative optimization.

Sightline differentiation:

- Make the workflow self-serve and visual.
- Lead with simulation and pre-flight review, not managed-services analytics.
- Score the specific creative inside the specific physical context.
- Produce a client-ready rationale before a placement is bought.

Sources:
- https://www.billups.com/products/analytics
- https://www.billups.com/products/billups-analytics/attention-dashboard

### Kinetic / GroupM Sightline

Strategic threat level: high for naming, medium for product overlap.

Kinetic already uses "Sightline" for GroupM's advanced programmatic DOOH solution suite, developed with Xaxis. It focuses on planning, executing, measuring, and integrating DOOH into omnichannel programmatic campaigns.

Implication:

- The current product name has a serious collision in the exact same industry.
- If this becomes more than a demo, rename or strongly qualify the brand.
- The product should avoid sounding like a programmatic DOOH suite.

Source: https://kineticww.com/sightline/

### AdQuick

Strategic threat level: medium-high.

AdQuick is a plan-buy-measure OOH platform with inventory access, campaign execution, APIs, reporting, and attribution workflows. It is stronger after the buyer is ready to transact.

Sightline differentiation:

- Sit upstream of AdQuick: decide if a creative/location is worth buying.
- Export placement/creative rationale that could feed a marketplace or agency brief.
- Do not claim better inventory coverage or pricing.

Sources:
- https://www.adquick.com/
- https://docs.adquick.com/

### Vistar / Broadsign / Place Exchange / Hivestack / VIOOH

Strategic threat level: medium.

These are infrastructure competitors, not direct product competitors. They own programmatic pipes, screen monetization, ad serving, supply integrations, targeting, and reporting. Vistar and Broadsign are especially important because they shape buyer expectations for modern DOOH.

Sightline differentiation:

- Avoid DSP/SSP language.
- Position as a planning and QA layer that can recommend what to buy and why.
- Long-term integration path: export scored candidates into programmatic or marketplace workflows.

Sources:
- https://www.vistarmedia.com/programmatic-marketplace
- https://broadsign.com/broadsign-platform
- https://www.placeexchange.com/
- https://future.hivestack.com/
- https://www.viooh.com/

### Veridooh

Strategic threat level: medium.

Veridooh focuses on independent verification, campaign automation, reporting, and intelligence across digital, programmatic, and static OOH. Its value is proof that campaigns ran as planned.

Sightline differentiation:

- Veridooh answers "did it run correctly?"
- Sightline answers "should we run this creative here in the first place?"

Source: https://www.veridooh.com/

### Geopath and Ipsos/Route

Strategic threat level: medium as authority, low as direct workflow competitor.

These are audience measurement standards and research systems. Geopath is the U.S. industry standard for OOH audience/location measurement. Route/Ipsos is important in audience measurement methodology.

Sightline differentiation:

- Do not present synthetic scores as official impressions.
- Use language like "pre-flight proxy," "relative comparison," "visibility/readability estimate," and "creative QA."
- Later partnership path: combine official reach/frequency with Sightline's visual quality scores.

Sources:
- https://geopath.org/
- https://support.geopath.io/hc/en-us/articles/360001845811-Methodology
- https://www.ipsos.com/sites/default/files/2020-04/route_a_methodological_overview.pdf

### Blip and BillboardsIn

Strategic threat level: low-medium.

These are self-serve/local buying competitors. They matter because they set a buyer expectation that billboard buying can be simple. Blip emphasizes launching campaigns quickly with flexible spend and no long-term contracts. BillboardsIn emphasizes available options, vendor competition, and expert support.

Sightline differentiation:

- Do not try to beat them at "launch in minutes."
- Use them as proof that SMB/local buyers want easier OOH access.
- Sightline could be an upstream creative/placement checker for buyers before they spend on these platforms.

Sources:
- https://www.blipbillboards.com/
- https://www.billboardsin.com/

### OUTFRONT, Lamar, JCDecaux, Clear Channel Outdoor

Strategic threat level: medium as data/supply owners, low as direct software competitors.

Large media owners control inventory and provide their own audience tools. OUTFRONT's smartSCOUT and audience measurement messaging, for example, combines proprietary data with location, roadway, mobile, and syndicated audience sources. Lamar highlights a large U.S. digital billboard network.

Sightline differentiation:

- Treat media owners as future data/API partners.
- Do not imply vendor-neutral availability, pricing, or booking unless integrations exist.
- Show how Sightline improves creative quality and placement confidence across any owner.

Sources:
- https://www.outfront.com/ad-tech/audience-measurement
- https://lamar.com/en/about

## Positioning Recommendation

Use:

> Sightline is a pre-flight simulator and creative QA layer for out-of-home advertising.

Avoid:

- "OOH marketplace"
- "Programmatic DOOH platform"
- "Official audience measurement"
- "Attribution platform"
- "End-to-end booking"

## Product Claims That Are Defensible Now

- Estimate whether a placement is visually strong before buying it.
- Compare creative variants in the context of a specific location.
- Detect obvious readability, contrast, copy-length, QR, and obstruction risks.
- Simulate likely exposure paths and explain the result.
- Export a client-ready pre-flight report.

## Product Claims To Avoid Until Integrated

- Official impressions.
- Real reach/frequency.
- Real audience demographics.
- Real availability/pricing.
- Direct booking.
- Footfall or sales lift.
- Proof-of-play or proof-of-posting.
- Industry-standard measurement currency.

## Best Wedge

The best initial buyer is an agency strategist, SMB marketer, or brand team that already has an OOH idea but lacks confidence before spending.

The first paid workflow should be:

1. Upload or generate creative.
2. Choose or import a candidate placement.
3. Run pre-flight simulation.
4. Receive a Sightline Score with visible evidence.
5. Compare variants.
6. Export a report for approval or vendor brief.

This is meaningfully different from buying platforms and measurement platforms because it improves the decision before campaign money is committed.
